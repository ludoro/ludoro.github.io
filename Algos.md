# Chip Huyen ML interview book solutions.
I have been reading [this](https://huyenchip.com/ml-interviews-book/) book by
Chip Huyen. In my opinion, It's a super well written book for graduates that want
to break into ML roles in industry. The provided questions are really nice, but
the book lacks solution. Given that I am preparing for interviews, It looks like
a fun exercise to work through all of them to the best of my ability.

# Algorithms for coding interviews

This is topic with many different opinions on the best way to go about preparing.
Personally, I really like problems from:

- [Binarysearch](https://binarysearch.com)
- [AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp)
- [Codeforces](https://codeforces.com)
- [AlgoMonster](https://algo.monster)

I try to solve as many problems as possible *without* looking at solutions.
If I read a problem and I immediately know how to solve it, I just skip it.
That's why you are not going to find super easy questions here.
Sometimes a problem can be in different sections, the decision on where to put it
is totally *random*.
