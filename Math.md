## Quant research interviews questions

Below some useful book questions to know for upcoming quant finance interviews.
I have used the following books/resources to prepare:
- "Heard on the street" by Timothy Falcon Crack
- "A practical guide to financial interviews" by Xinfeng Zhou
- "Quant job interviews questions and answers" by Joshi et al.
- "An interviewer primer for quantitative finance" by Dirk Bester
- "Data science prep volume I,II and III" by [datascienceprep](https://datascienceprep.com/)
- "150 Most Frequently Asked Questions on Quant Interviews" by Dan Stefanica et al.
- "Probability and stochastic calculus quant interview questions" by Dan Stefanica et al.

For the theory, I feel like Stochastic calculus for finance I / II by Shreve are
really good.

If you are interested in accessing the breadth of questions contained in those
books, the best way to go about it is to, well, purchase those books.
If instead, you want to understand *my own take* on different interview topics,
then feel free to read below.

What I suggest doing for a fruitful preparation, is to not just iterate through
all books you can find and solve every questions mindlessly.
Interview questions are always changing so you cannot bruteforce your way through them.
Instead, I will quote the website [algomonster](https://algo.monster/):


>Learn patterns. The internet has no short supply of problems, however like an unsolved puzzle it can be a daunting task to see the big picture and patterns. Luckily interviews are centered around a small set of patterns. These are repeated over and over. Learn these building blocks, you’ll be able to apply them to any problem.


The argument here is about coding questions, but I feel that applies to many
different things. Still, the more problems solved, the better!

With that in mind, below I compiled the most useful tricks one needs to know,
alongside some questions to showcase those tricks.
